What can I do?

  • Protect your family

    Educate your child on water safety and don’t let them play alone in and near open waters. The Robert Hattersley foundation is trying to promote alternative, safer play for local children, and is trying to create a culture of awareness about the dangers of water.

    s

  • Protect your community

    The Robert Hattersley Foundation is creating a culture of awareness about the dangers of open waters in the Tyne and Wear area. You can promote this by discussing water safety with your child, by not letting them give into peer pressure, and by supporting alternative play and socialisation places.

  • Working with the Robert Hattersly Foundation

    We are an outwards looking organisation and we can always do with a helping hand. We especially need social media and safeguarding leads however anyone willing to volenteer time, skills and effort would be more than welcome.

Working with the RNLI

Swim Safe are free, guided swimming lessons for ages 7-14 taught by the RNLI. Learn more about Swim Safe on the RNLI website.

The RNLI offer an immense range of resources for education, including activity booklets, online learning programs and certificates. Discover RNLI's resources on their website..

The RNLI visits schools to educate and inform children about water safety, just like the Robert Hattersley Foundation. Learn more on their website.
